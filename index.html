Ada Lovelace

1815 - 1852

"The brain of mine is something more than merely mortal; as time will show."

Biography

Augusta Ada King, Countess of Lovelace, was an English mathematician and writer, chiefly known for her work on Charles Babbage’s early mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine.

Ada Lovelace is widely regarded as the world’s first computer programmer because she published the first algorithm intended to be processed by a machine. Her visionary notes described how machines could go beyond calculation and create music, art, and text.

Her ideas laid the foundation for modern computing more than a century before computers were actually built.
